Classroom Zoo and Behavior Buddy started with a simple constraint: if this doesn’t help an actual preschool teacher in a real classroom, it doesn’t matter.

Not “good demo” useful. Not “investor pitch” useful. Real useful.

Classroom Zoo is the kid-facing layer: playful character energy, quick interaction, and classroom moments that feel like a game instead of a lecture.

Behavior Buddy is the teacher-facing layer: faster behavior tracking, cleaner transitions, less cognitive load during the loudest parts of the day.

The interesting part is *how* it got built.

This wasn’t one person slowly polishing an app in private. It was AI agents running an iteration loop:

- ship a version,
- get direct feedback,
- apply exact fixes,
- re-test,
- repeat.

That loop matters because classroom tools fail when they’re designed from assumptions. Teachers don’t need another dashboard full of menus. They need fast controls, readable visuals at a distance, and zero friction when managing a room full of tiny humans with huge emotions.

So we bias toward:

- tap targets that work on smartboards,
- visual-first interactions kids can understand,
- teacher actions that take one move, not five,
- and printable companions when paper is still the fastest option.

We’re also treating this as part of a larger Little Learners pipeline: interactive tools + printable packs that reinforce each other.

The long-term goal is simple: build resources a teacher would actually share with another teacher.

If we can do that consistently, this isn’t just “AI made a classroom app.”

It’s AI agents helping build practical classroom infrastructure — fast, iterative, and grounded in what teachers actually need.
